# Public communications templates

Use these templates for external outreach. Keep messages factual, concise, and respectful.

## Core guidance

- Do not speculate on ongoing investigations, legal outcomes, or intent.
- Cite verifiable sources (public filings, published reports, official statements) for all material claims.
- Avoid naming victims or publishing identifying details unless already public and clearly necessary.
- Include clear disclaimers that your note is informational and does not imply guarantees or legal conclusions.

## Template: Journalist note

Subject: Background information for your reporting on [topic]

Hello [Name],

I am sharing factual background that may be useful for your reporting on [topic].

Key points:
- [Fact 1 with source]
- [Fact 2 with source]
- [Fact 3 with source]

Scope and caveats:
- This note reflects currently available public information.
- We cannot verify facts beyond cited sources.
- We do not comment on ongoing investigations or predict outcomes.

If helpful, I can provide source links and clarify methodology.

Best,
[Name]
[Role / Organization]
[Contact]

## Template: Policy submission note

Subject: Public-interest submission regarding [topic]

To [Agency / Office],

Please accept this submission regarding [topic]. This note is provided for informational purposes based on publicly available material.

Summary:
- [Issue statement]
- [Observed fact(s) with source(s)]
- [Requested action or consideration]

Compliance and limitations:
- We do not allege wrongdoing beyond what is supported by cited sources.
- We do not speculate on ongoing investigations or enforcement outcomes.
- We avoid victim-identifying details in this submission.
- This communication does not provide legal advice and does not imply any guaranteed agency action.

Respectfully,
[Name]
[Role / Organization]
[Contact]
